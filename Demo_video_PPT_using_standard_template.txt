Hard rule : Video Duration: Maximum 10 Minutes.Create a compelling demo video that effectively showcases and positions your idea or proof of concept (POC) to the client—focusing on clear value articulation, persuasive storytelling, and influencing customer decision-making through strong marketing and recommendation messaging.

Evaluation Criteria’s

Relevance to Challenge (20 Points)	•	Explain how your solution directly addresses the challenge statement. Show clear alignment between the problem and your approach
Innovation & Creativity (10 Points)	•	Highlight what makes your approach novel. What existing solutions did you improve upon or what new technique did you introduce?
Technical Implementation (10 Points)	Describe your tech stack, architecture decisions, and key integrations. Show that you built something functional, not just a concept
User Experience (15 Points)	•	Explain how users interact with your solution. Focus on intuitive design, accessibility, and reduced friction in the workflow
Impact & Scalability (15 Points)	•	Quantify potential impact (e.g., time saved, cost reduced) and explain how your solution can scale across teams, regions, or industries
Presentation & Pitch (10 Points)	•	Structure your pitch: Problem → Solution → Demo → Impact → Roadmap. Keep it concise and lead with the business value
Adaptability & Flexibility (10 Points)	•	Show how your solution can be adapted to other use cases or industries beyond the initial challenge scope
Overall Impression (10 Points)	•	Ensure professional polish: clean slides, consistent design, working demo, and a compelling narrative that ties everything together
Why does your POC/Tool worthy of GTM development ? - Articulate market demand, competitive advantage, and a clear path from POC to production-ready product


Demo Video & Pitch Best Practices

Demo Video Structure (Max 10 Minutes)
Minutes 1-2: Hook with the problem. Show the pain point with a relatable scenario
Minutes 2-4: Live demo of your working solution. Show the happy path end-to-end
Minutes 4-6: Architecture walkthrough. Explain your tech choices and Microsoft service integrations
Minutes 6-8: Business impact and scalability. Quantify the value
Minutes 8-10: Future roadmap and GTM potential. Close with a strong call to action


Pitch Presentation Tips
Lead with the “why” — start with the problem and the people affected, not the technology
Use storytelling: Before vs. After your solution. Paint a clear picture of the transformation
Keep slides visual and minimal — avoid text-heavy slides. Use diagrams, screenshots, and data visuals
Rehearse as a team. Assign speaking roles and practice transitions between speakers
End with confidence — summarize key value, restate the problem-solution fit, and outline next steps


Scoring Maximizer Checklist
Relevance (20 pts): Explicit mapping between challenge statement and your solution approach
UX (15 pts) + Impact (15 pts): Show a user-friendly interface with measurable outcomes
Innovation (10 pts) + Tech (10 pts): Novel approach with solid, well-integrated Microsoft stack
Adaptability (10 pts) + Overall (10 pts): Show extensibility and leave a lasting impression
